Designing immersive learning environments for embodied, collaborative, and data-informed education.

I am a Ph.D. student in Educational Psychology at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, advised by Dr. Mitchell Nathan. My work examines how learners use gestures, mixed reality manipulatives, and multimodal interactions to reason, collaborate, and transfer mathematical ideas.

Research agenda

How learning unfolds through bodies, tools, and social interaction.

01

Embodied geometric reasoning

Comparing physical, augmented reality, and mixed reality manipulatives to understand how students and teachers generate, categorize, and transfer geometric ideas.

02

Collaborative XR learning

Studying how learners ground mathematical meaning through distributed gestures and shared representations in extended reality learning simulations.

03

Multimodal learning analytics

Linking eye tracking, facial expression, gesture, log data, and discourse to cognitive and affective states in simulation-based learning.

04

AI and simulation design

Designing and evaluating AI chatbots, adaptive simulations, and participatory design processes for STEM and teacher education contexts.
